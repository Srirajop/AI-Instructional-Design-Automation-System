Screen 4.2 Title: Types of Bias in AI Systems

On-Screen Text (OST) | Audio Narration | Visual Instructions & Developer Notes
• Intentional bias is when developers intentionally design the system to favor certain groups. • Unintentional bias occurs when the system learns patterns from biased data. • Confirmation bias is when the system only considers information that confirms its existing beliefs. • Anchoring bias is when the system relies too heavily on the first piece of information it receives. | Narration: "There are different types of bias in AI systems, including intentional and unintentional bias. Intentional bias is when developers intentionally design the system to favor certain groups. This can be done to achieve a specific goal or to manipulate the outcome of the system. Unintentional bias, on the other hand, occurs when the system learns patterns from biased data. This can happen when the training data is biased or when the system is not properly designed. Additionally, confirmation bias and anchoring bias can also affect the performance of AI systems. Confirmation bias occurs when the system only considers information that confirms its existing beliefs, while anchoring bias occurs when the system relies too heavily on the first piece of information it receives." | Graphics: A diagram showing the different types of bias in AI systems.
